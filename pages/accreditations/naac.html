ACCREDITATION IMPACT

Why NAAC Grade Controls Degree Values

The National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) is an autonomous council established by the University Grants Commission (UGC) to assess and grade higher learning portals. A grade of NAAC A++ (CGPA 3.51 - 4.00) or A+ (CGPA 3.26 - 3.50) indicates elite educational operations, high teaching quality, top-tier research, and highly secure digital infrastructures.

For online students, a university holding NAAC A++ grade validity is highly valued. This certification ensures that your digital library, recorded video catalogs, proctored examinations platforms, and mentor resources meet absolute quality thresholds. Consequently, top global MNCs and government recruiting cells prioritize NAAC A+ or A++ graduates in screening steps.

SCORING BENCHMARKS

Key NAAC Assessment Pillars

The 5 structural criteria analyzed by NAAC panels during multi-stage evaluation audits.

01

Curricular Design & Aspect

Examines syllabus structures, industry integration variables, elective choices, and global equivalence matching.

02

Teaching-Learning & Evaluation

Audits faculty qualifications, interactive learning methodologies, digital library scopes, and web exam proctoring setups.

03

Infrastructure & Learning Portals

Evaluates LMS dashboards speeds, virtual classroom grids availability, self-paced archives, and mobile compatibility.

04

Student Support & Progression

Measures placement assistance cells success rates, alumni counseling, zero-cost EMI options, and scholarship portals.

Governance & Institutional Values

Reviews standard academic administration integrity, fee structures transparency, and regulatory guidelines compliance.
