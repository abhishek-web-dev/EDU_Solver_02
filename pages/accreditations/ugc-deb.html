REGULATORY AUTHORITY

Gazette Section 22: Online Degrees carry 100% Parity

Every online degree program offered in India MUST possess active, verified approvals from the Distance Education Bureau (DEB) of the University Grants Commission (UGC). Without this validation, a degree is legally invalid for corporate promotions, government job examinations, or migration credentials.

Under official UGC notifications, online and distance degrees completed at approved higher education campuses hold absolute parity and are legally at par with conventional physical campus regular degrees for all recruitment and job purposes. This guarantees that an online degree recipient can qualify for competitive exams (like UPSC, SSC, Banking, and PSUs) and secure global credential certifications seamlessly.

How to Verify UGC-DEB Approvals

Follow these 5 formal verification steps on the official government portals to confirm active ODL cohort validation.

01

Access official UGC-DEB Portal

Visit the official portal at deb.ugc.ac.in to open active university status trackers.

02

Select Academic Cohort Year

Select the exact academic year (e.g. 2025-2026) matching your target enrollment timeline.

03

Search University State & Name

Locate the institution by filtering details using their head campus state (e.g. Punjab, Uttar Pradesh).

04

Check Approved Program Lists

Ensure the specific stream (e.g. MBA, MCA) is listed under their officially approved distance/online catalog.

Download Official Approval Letter

Lock in security by downloading the university's official PDF validation circular for record-keeping.
